What is National Cashflow Competition?

National Cashflow Competition features the use of the CASHFLOW 101 board game created by Robert Kiyosaki, author of best-seller “Rich Dad Poor Dad”. An exciting game, stimulating life as a rat race and teaches practical finance knowledge on wealth management to players.

What can you expect?

- Fundamentals of various investment concepts
- Trained in financial literacy
- Hone valuable financial skills
- Fun learning experience with friends and facilitators

What is Singapore Financial Conference?

Singapore Financial Conference is an annual event organized by NTU Investment Interactive Club, seeking to give our participants an opportunity to gain valuable financial insights from the nation’s top investing gurus. The conference includes sharing sessions with distinguished speakers from different fields who would share their financial expertise on a wide range of investment topics and career advice.

FINEX

Financial Expedition, FINEX in short, is a team-based expedition challenge similar to the Amazing Race. Since 2009, FINEX aims to prepare youths with the necessary financial skills and knowledge. Through a series of games and activities, students get to learn key financial concepts involving investments, budgeting and trade-offs.

About Us

Background

NTU Investment Interactive Club (IIC) is a non-profit club founded in 1999 in response to the government’s call for initiatives to develop a vibrant investing community. Since then, NTU-IIC has grown substantially to become the largest finance society in NTU today.

Vision

Our club vision,
“Towards a Knowledgeable Investing Student Population”
is the driving force behind all our projects. IIC plans to realize this vision through three objectives:
  • Promote greater awareness for the need of investment especially among young students and at the same time develop students' interest in financial market.
  • Assist IIC members to develop and further hone their practical investing skills and strategies.
  • Foster close interactions between various educational institutions and the financial community.
